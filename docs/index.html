HydrometBench

The CIROH Hydrologic Prediction Testbed

About HydrometBench

HydrometBench is a community-oriented initiative to establish rigorous, quantitative intercomparison and benchmarking of US operational hydrologic forecasts, and particularly the multiple elements – models, methods, datasets – involved in producing them.

The testbed’s overarching goal is to address the problematic lack of coherence of research into fundamental challenges and needs for operational prediction systems, which is a significant impediment to intercomparison, benchmarking, and synergistic learning across diverse investments into forecasting research and development.

The testbed promotes a multi-faceted benchmark-oriented paradigm through facilitating the use of multiple community-based experimental protocols with standardized evaluation tools, targeting different forecasting and forecasting sub-component objectives across timescales from nowcasting to multi-season prediction.

Current Focus

Protocols are the heart of the testbed. Our current focus includes streamflow simulation, short-range forecasting, snow water equivalent analysis, and climate prediction at multiple timescales.

The Process

The TEEHR (Tools for Exploratory Evaluation in Hydrologic Research) framework powers our evaluation pipeline, enabling standardized performance assessments across diverse modeling approaches. The testbed is designed for quick turnaround in a forecasting loop, enabling both rapid exploratory evaluation and formal protocol-based assessments.

Key Features

Benchmarking

Rigorous, quantitative intercomparison of US operational hydrologic forecasts and the models, methods, and datasets that produce them.

Protocols

Community-based experimental protocols with standardized evaluation tools targeting different forecasting objectives.

Collaboration

Open engagement for CIROH teams and external collaborators to assess innovations against benchmark baselines.

How to Participate

  1. Define Protocol — Establish standardized benchmarks, observation datasets, and performance metrics for evaluation.
  2. Execute Models — Run hydrologic simulations across defined spatial and temporal domains with consistent configurations.
  3. Analyze Results — Evaluate model performance using metrics like KGE, NSE, rBias, and flow threshold statistics.
  4. Publish & Collaborate — Submit results to the testbed and share findings with the community.
